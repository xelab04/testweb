Education

College du St Esprit has been historically recognised for its quality of education. While not the sole focus of the college, it is undeniable that the quality of education at CSE is among the best on the island.

More Info.

Sports

College du St Esprit has always had a very distinct prominence in everything sports-related, from the annual college's cross country to intercolleges. We firmly believe in promoting sports to the same extent as academics.

Clubs

Clubs are created and managed entirely by students. We have a massive variety of clubs covering co-curricular and extra-curricular domains. Some of our largest clubs are the Debating Club, Science Club and Coding Club.

Our History

The secondary school was founded in January 1938 by Fathers of the Congregation of The Holy Spirit. In 1920, The 'Séminaire Père Laval' was inaugurated and in 1926 'le Collège Père Laval' was founded with priests as teachers. However, due to a lack of priests, 'le Collège Père Laval' closed its doors and it was only in 1938 that the college re-opened its doors as le Collège du Saint Esprit. In 1950, the college obtained its first laureate (Roland LAMUSSE). In 1972, l’Abbé Adrien WIEHE became the first Mauritian rector of the school and four years later there was the beginning of the secular administration with Mr Cyril LECKNING as rector of the college.
